Fisher information (FI) is a commonly used measure of information in neuroscience. It is so fundamental that many papers just directly apply related results without fully explaining the underlying framework, and yet the concept of FI is also intricate enough to cause confusion for those who have only encountered it for a few times. Yes, you could go to Brunel and Nadal's work and read their original notations in detail, but these notations have been changed and simplified in later works, and for your purposes reading B&N could be an overkill. Here I will attempt to explain how FI is related to concepts like neural tuning, representational geometry, perceptual discrimination, etc., with an emphasis on filling in oft-omitted derivations (you will see that they are indeed simple, but not obvious) and explicitly highlighting connections among concepts in FI's neighborhood. The framework I use is based on Kriegeskorte and Wei's 2021 review paper, but the hope is that you do not have to read it to understand this post.

Fisher Information (FI) is defined as the expected precision of (an estimator of) stimulus $\theta$, and is formally expressed as:
\begin{align}
  J(\theta) = \int{-\frac{d^2}{d\theta}\log p(r|\theta)p(r)dr}
\end{align}
If we assume stimulus-independent Gaussian noise with variance 1, then FI is simply:
\begin{align}
  J(\theta) = f'(\theta)^2
\end{align}
, where f(\theta) is the tuning function.
If we assume Poisson spiking, then FI can be shown to have the following form instead:
\begin{align}
  J(\theta) = \frac{f'(\theta)^2}{f(\theta)}
\end{align}

Proof for (2):
When p(r|\theta) is Gaussian,
\begin{align}
  \log p(r|theta) = -\frac{(r-f(\theta))^2}{2\sigma^2} + const \
  J(\theta) &= -\mathbb{E}_{r|\theta}[\frac{d^2}{d\theta^2}\log p(r|\theta)] \
            &= -\mathbb{E}_{r|\theta}[\frac{d^2}{d\theta^2}(\frac{rf(\theta)}{\sigma^2}-\frac{f(\theta)^2}{2\sigma^2})] \
            &= -\mathbb{E}_{r|\theta}[\frac{rf''(\theta)}{\sigma^2}-(\frac{2f(\theta)f'(\theta)}{2\sigma^2})'] \
            &= -\mathbb{E}_{r|\theta}[\frac{rf''(\theta)}{\sigma^2}-\frac{f'(\theta)^2}{\sigma^2}-\frac{f(\theta)f''(\theta)}{\sigma^2}] \
            &\text{since} -\mathbb{E}_{r|\theta}(r) = f(\theta)\
            &= -\mathbb{E}[-\frac{1}{\sigma^2}f'(\theta)^2]\
  \text{assuming $\sigma$ = 1}\
  J(\theta) &= f'(\theta)^2
\end{align}
