PhD student of neuroscience at [NYU](https://shanghai.nyu.edu/academics/graduate/neural-science-phd-program) (Shanghai track, advised by [Prof. Sukbin Lim](https://shanghai.nyu.edu/academics/faculty/directory/sukbin-lim)), and recent graduate of [UChicago](https://masters.cs.uchicago.edu/) computer science (advised by [Prof. Yuxin Chen](https://yuxinchen.org)). My research interests include: computational neuroscience, artificial intelligence, and machine learning. As a broad goal, I care about acpects from these fields that would benefit our understanding of the human brain. At the same time I have an inclination towards abstract formulations and ideas. So I try to strike a balance between these.

I'm fascinated by the neural or feature representations inside our brains, and I am passionate about biologically plausible models of neural networks. I hope to leverage and develop machine learning methods to inform research on either or both these areas.

Additionally, I am also interested in how machines can augment human cognition and in general, how the two distinct kinds of intelligence can efficiently interact. I have worked on the machine learning theory topic of "machine teaching" where we explored how humans or machines could be efficient in teaching and influencing a machine learner.
